The United States has 61 protected areas known as national parks that are operated by the National Park Service, an agency of the Department of the Interior.
National parks must be established by an act of the United States Congress. A bill creating the first national park, Yellowstone, was signed into law by President Ulysses S. Grant in 1872.
Fourteen national parks are designated UNESCO World Heritage Sites (WHS), while 21 national parks are designated UNESCO Biosphere Reserves (BR).Eight national parks are designated in both UNESCO programs.
